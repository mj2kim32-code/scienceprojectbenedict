    • Background
    Hydrogen is the simplest and most common element in the universe, usually found as a gas made of two atoms. It has an atomic number of 1 and comes in three types called isotopes: protonium, deuterium, and tritium. On Earth, hydrogen is mostly found in compounds like water and can be separated using methods like electrolysis or other chemical reactions. It is very important in chemistry, especially for making ammonia, and it is also being studied as a clean energy source that can power fuel cells and engines, with the possibility of being used in fusion energy in the future. However, hydrogen can be hard to store and use because it has a very low boiling point and can react dangerously with oxygen. Today, scientists are working on ways to produce hydrogen using renewable energy to make it a more sustainable energy option. –(Hydrogen H Ebsco Research)

    Reaction rates tell us how fast a chemical reaction happens, or how quickly reactants turn into products. The speed of a reaction can change depending on things like how much reactant there is, the temperature, pressure, and if a catalyst is used. When there are more particles, they collide more often, making the reaction faster. Scientists study reaction rates by measuring how substances change over time. Sometimes reactions reach equilibrium, where everything stays balanced because the forward and backward reactions happen at the same speed. Understanding reaction rates is important in real life, especially in biology, like how the body processes food and medicine. –(Reaction Rates Ebsco Research)
